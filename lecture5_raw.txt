◻Uses a register instead of a constant to specify the 16-bit

Based Addressing Mode 
[X]Operand is located at the address given by adding 8 or 16 
bit displacement to either BX or BP and combing the result 
with a segment register 
[X]Displacement must be specified in the operand field 
[X]Interpreted as signed 2’s complement value 
[X]Examples 
[X]Assume DS=10000h, SS=20000h, BP=0222h, BX=0111h 
[X]MOV AX, [BP-2] ; Copy the content of 20220h and 20221h to AX 
[X]MOV [BX+777h], AX ; Copy AL to 10888h and AH to 10889h 46

Based-Index-Relative Addressing 
[X]The base and index registers are added to give the 
segment offset of where the operand is located 
[X]The base register (either BX or BP) is added to an index 
register(DI or SI) as positive integers only 
[X]Each register lies in the range 0 to 65535 
[X]By default, the segment address is derived from DS except 
the BP register, which is derived from SS 
[X]A signed displacement may also be included to calculate the

String Addressing Mode 
◻A string is a series of bytes or words in sequential memory 
locations 
◻String instructions do not use any of the previous address 
modes 
◻Strings may be up to 64KB in length 
◻String addressing modes uses SI, DI, DS, and ES registers 
◻String instructions assume SI points to first byte of string to 
be processed, and DI points to first byte of destination string 
◻The use of a register my be implicit in the instruction 51

--- PAGE 53 ---
Port Addressing Mode 
[X]8086 has separate input/output space 
[X]Up to 65536 I/O ports are available 
[X]The I/O ports may be addressed by a byte sized constant 
[X]Limited to I/O ports in the range 0 to 255 
[X]IN AL, 40h ; Put the content of I/O port 40h into AL 
[X]OUT 80h, AL ; Send the contents of AL to I/O port 80h 
[X]The I/O ports may be addressed using a register 
[X]Full range of 65536 ports are accessible 
[X]IN AL, DX ; Load AL with the byte from port address given by DX 
[X]OUT DX, AX ; Send the word in AX to port address given by DX 53
